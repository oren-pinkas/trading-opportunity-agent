## Round 1 - Independent research

### Bull (sonnet)

Long TSM, small size via short-dated calls/call-spread. Entry ~Aug 10-11 near
$415.20; exit within 1-3 trading days post-Aug-12 event. Thesis: not a
Pixel-revenue play (concedes Pixel unit volumes are a rounding error against
TSMC's ~$90B+ revenue base) but a narrative-reinforcement/momentum trade - a
dated, retail/tech-media-visible spotlight moment showcasing working 2nm
silicon, in a stock trading heavily on "is the AI/leading-edge moat intact."
Confidence ~35%. Flags its own Pixel-share estimate as an estimate, not a hard
number. States the IPO-data-risk lesson does not apply - TSM has traded since
1997, deeply liquid, deep options market.

### Bear (sonnet)

No-trade. Pixel is under 1% of global smartphone units (Counterpoint/IDC-consistent
estimate) vs iPhone's 200M+, a rounding error against TSMC's revenue dominated by
Apple/Nvidia/AMD/HPC-AI. The 2nm/AI-capex narrative is already priced in via
TSMC's own earnings commentary across multiple quarters - this is a GOOGL story,
not a TSM story. Named risks: (1) single-source speculative preview journalism,
not confirmed by Google or TSMC, real risk of date/spec slippage; (2) event is
~3 weeks out, calendar risk; (3) TSM price is dominated by its own earnings, AI
capex trends, and export-policy headlines, which will swamp and make
unfalsifiable any Pixel-attributable signal. Agrees the IPO-data-risk lesson does
not apply; flags only that entry/exit minute should avoid illiquid
pre/after-hours prints generically (non-issue for TSM in regular hours). Would
reconsider given a TSMC-attributed statement tying Pixel to material 2nm capacity
share, or evidence of N2 supply constraints pulling forward revenue recognition.

### Quant (opus)

No position, 0% sizing. Base rate for an event-attributable >1% TSM move on a
scheduled, pre-telegraphed phone launch: ~5%. Reasoning: ~10M Pixel units/yr at
~$80-100/die implies ~$0.8-1.0B of TSMC revenue against ~$120B+ annual revenue -
under 1%, incremental surprise a rounding error. P(direction correct if traded):
~50%, no directional edge. EV = P(move) x [P(dir) x gain - P(wrong) x loss] -
costs; assuming 1.5% conditional move and 7bps round-trip cost, EV ~ -7bps even
under a generous 55%-directional-lean case. 2nm ramp already guided on earnings
calls for quarters - textbook already-priced-consensus, no consensus-estimate
revision mechanism from one low-volume SKU. Recommendation: no position, 0%
sizing; would only reconsider on a genuine surprise (foundry-share shift, 2nm
yield/pricing headline, unexpected volume commitment). Confirms IPO-data-risk
lesson is irrelevant - TSM's real reference print ($415.20 at
2026-07-23T19:59:00Z) shows clean, continuous minute-level coverage, no stub-data
risk.

## Round 2 - Rebuttal

### Bull (sonnet)

Concedes: bear is right on magnitude (Pixel rounding error, no Pixel-revenue
thesis); quant is right that a directional-equity EV calc is negative. Pushes
back: reframes as a short-dated options/momentum-vol play on a dated retail-media
spotlight, not a fundamentals bet - claims convexity/attention-clustering around
a known date isn't captured by quant's linear, symmetric-odds EV model. But
explicitly admits this edge is "thin, hard to verify," and has NOT verified
whether prior comparable Google hardware launches produced any measurable TSM
vol bump. Shrinks hard: at most a token, defined-risk, tiny options structure
framed as a lottery ticket; leans toward conceding to no-trade unless an
(unperformed) historical vol-bump check came back supportive.

### Bear (sonnet)

No transmission mechanism identified from consumer/retail attention to
institutional order flow - TSM's float is overwhelmingly institutional/quant,
priced on wafer starts, capex guidance, ASP trends, customer mix, not tech-media
sentiment. Argues quant's 5% base rate is generous, not conservative: this event
has no earnings/guidance component, so true conditional probability is closer to
2-3%, making EV more negative than -7bps. Flags a concrete structural risk in the
bull's proposed options trade: IV crush - a widely-telegraphed date bids up
short-dated IV pre-event and collapses it post-event ("sell the news" on vol),
which can erase gains within the bull's own 1-3 day exit window even if
directionally right. Still no-trade; would only reconsider given concrete
historical precedent of TSM vol spiking around comparable single-customer
consumer launches (none offered).

### Quant (opus)

Options reframe does not rescue EV - IV crush and theta decay on short-dated
premium bought into a pre-telegraphed date make EV more negative, not less;
defined-risk (call-spread) caps the loss shape but not the negative sign. Bear's
unfalsifiability point doesn't move the 5% base-rate number itself but kills any
epistemic yield from the trade - can't attribute or learn from the outcome even
if TSM pops on Aug 12, since macro/earnings/export headlines dominate the tape.
Numeric verdict: 80/100 confidence that no-trade dominates over the bull's small
speculative structure; the remaining 20 points reserved only for a
mandate-level question (whether sub-10bps defined-risk speculative lotteries are
explicitly carved out as acceptable portfolio noise - not the case here). Final:
holds at 0% sizing, options framing makes the no-trade case stronger, not weaker.

## Round 3 - Convergence (synthesizer, opus)

**Hypothesis:** The Aug 12 2026 "Made by Google" Pixel 11 / 2nm Tensor G6 launch
is not a tradable catalyst for TSM. Pixel volume is a rounding error (under 1%
of TSMC revenue), the 2nm/AI-capex narrative is already priced via TSMC's own
earnings guidance, there is no identified transmission mechanism from
retail/tech-media attention to institution-dominated TSM order flow, and the sole
source is a single unconfirmed Digital Trends preview whose date/specs could
slip. A directional EV calc is negative (~-7bps on a ~5% event-attributable
>1%-move base rate, ~50% direction accuracy); the short-dated options reframe
worsens EV via IV crush and theta decay into a pre-telegraphed date. Any TSM move
over the window would be macro/AI-capex/earnings-driven and unfalsifiable as a
Pixel signal, yielding no epistemic value even on a favorable outcome.

- Direction: no-trade
- Confidence: 82

**Plan:** No trade. Ticker TSM, action no-trade, no entry/exit/expected profit.

**Dissent (for post-mortem):** The bull's residual claim that a short-dated
options/momentum-vol structure could capture a narrative-reinforcement "spotlight"
bump around the event - held only as a tiny defined-risk lottery ticket - was
never validated, since no vol base-rate check on comparable prior
Google/single-customer hardware launches was performed. Bear and quant reject
this outright - IV crush and theta make EV more negative, not less; no
historical precedent of TSM vol spiking around consumer launches was ever
offered; institutional float shows no transmission channel. Unresolved for the
post-mortem: would a vol base-rate study of prior Pixel/comparable launches have
surfaced any measurable, tradable TSM IV or realized-vol signature, or confirmed
its absence. Absent that evidence the bull could not defend even the stub, and
no-trade dominates.
