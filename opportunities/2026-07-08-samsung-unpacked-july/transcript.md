echo), so cost estimates don't carry an extra liquidity penalty. Concedes DS is ~94%

earnings leg rather than a pure product-launch trade.

### Bear
Argues the bull's Feb 2026 precedent is a different regime (full MX flagship cycle,
pre-supercycle-narrative dominance) vs. now, when the market just proved (July 7) it
sells profit beats on supercycle-peaking fears — comparing the two regimes is a
category error. The cited 24-61% analyst upside is a 12-month DS/HBM-driven price
target being used to validate a 48-hour event trade — also a category error. Pushes
back on the quant's symmetric framing: the leaked pricing-hike table is already-public
information pointing one direction (downside skew, not noise), and profit-mix caps how
much good MX news can help while DS/macro fear is an unbounded left tail. Argues
Unpacked (Jul 22) and earnings-call MX commentary (Jul 23) are likely correlated, not
independent draws, contra the quant's separable framing. CONCLUSION: no long (reject
bull), but also no standalone short (the edges point the same direction as cost
friction, not against it) — stand aside; if forced to lean on a pure Unpacked-only
bet, leans short not flat. Confidence in "avoid the long, stand aside": 80/100.

the 5-7% hold-through-earnings trade. Confidence in "no edge holding through both
catalysts": 76/100.

## Round 3 — synthesis

All three personas converged against the bull's original "hold through both
catalysts" trade. The quant's variance decomposition (earnings ≈93% of combined
variance) and the bear's regime-mismatch argument independently killed it; the bull's
own Round 2 concessions (confidence 55→42, size cut ~60%, relabeling as an
earnings-dominated trade) amount to a tacit admission. The isolated launch leg alone
is breakeven-to-negative net of cost in both directions, and the bear's short is
separately dead because the July 7 drop already priced in the skeptical thesis.

The quant's own narrow fallback — a small pre-event momentum long, exited flat before
July 22, contingent on a run-up appearing in the tape — does not fire today: the only
live tape signal referenced in this debate (the July 7 preliminary-guidance reaction)
was a sharp drop, not a run-up, so the fallback's entry condition is unmet as of
2026-07-10.

**Hypothesis:** No standalone tradeable edge exists in Samsung (005930.KS) around the
Unpacked/earnings catalyst pair. **Direction:** neutral. **Confidence:** 82.

**Plan:** no action. Ticker 005930.KS, action none, no entry/exit, expected_profit_pct
0. If a live multi-day positive drift in KRX-session closes appears between now and
~July 21, the quant's narrow pre-event-only variant should be re-evaluated as a fresh,
separate, non-earnings-touching trade — that is a contingency to monitor, not a plan
to execute today.

**Dissent:** The strongest unresolved disagreement, worth carrying into the
post-mortem: is the launch-leg return distribution symmetric noise (quant's N(0,0.8%)
framing) or negatively skewed (bear's position)? The bear argues the already-public
leaked pricing-hike table points one direction (downside), that MX profit-mix caps how
much good news can help while DS/macro fear is an unbounded left tail, and that the
Unpacked and earnings legs are correlated rather than independent draws — all of which
the quant's variance-decomposition model treats as separable, symmetric shocks.
Neither side operationalized this into a re-priced EV. If the bear's skew argument is
correct, it doesn't just kill the bull's long — it also invalidates the quant's own
"small pre-event long if run-up appears" fallback as mis-signed, since a
skewed-negative distribution would argue for fading a run-up, not riding it. This
should be the first thing the next research pass tries to resolve quantitatively
(e.g., by modeling the historical base-rate effect of pre-event pricing leaks on
launch-day returns) rather than leaving it as dueling priors.
