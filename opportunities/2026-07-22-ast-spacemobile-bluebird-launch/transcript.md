### Bear (confidence 25/100) — No-trade / mild short-the-pop bias
What's priced in: the launch was announced 2026-06-23, a full month before this dossier. A heavily-followed, high-short-interest, retail-momentum name has had four weeks to digest this; $61.67 already reflects the probability-weighted expectation. Risks: launch slip is common in the rideshare industry and the window is already a soft, month-old guidance; commissioning/deployment (solar array/antenna unfurl, orbit-raising, testing) takes weeks-to-months post-launch — the revenue-relevant milestone is well beyond 2026-08-15, so the impact window may capture only launch-day pop/fade, not durable fundamental shift; dilution/cash-burn risk typical of capital-intensive pre-revenue satellite companies; this is incremental (not the first BlueBird batch) — execution against a known plan, not transformative.

What bull is likely ignoring: the gap between "launch" and "monetizable capacity," and that a month-old, well-covered announcement has minimal remaining information edge.

What would change my mind: a confirmed near-term short-squeeze setup with unusually low pre-event IV, credible reporting that commercial service activation is tightly coupled to this batch, or the launch date getting pulled forward (a positive surprise vs. guidance).

## Round 2 — Rebuttals

### Bull rebuttal (revised confidence 52/100, position unchanged)
Bear conflates "known" with "resolved" — a genuinely priced-in catalyst would trade tight into the event, not sit at $61.67 with an unconfirmed exact date and a wide window; retail/momentum names re-rate on catalyst *confirmation*, not just announcement. Quant's EV math is the stronger challenge but is static where the trade is dynamic — the bull plan scales out around the launch and holds only a partial runner, sized to survive the 40% slip branch via the $56 stop; waiting for a confirmed date (quant's suggestion) isn't a free option since date-confirmation news typically gaps the stock. Concession: hadn't weighted bear's dilution/capital-raise-near-launch risk enough. What would change bull's mind: a slip announcement pushing past 08-15 (exit, don't average down), a break below $56 on volume pre-launch-news (setup invalidated), or a confirmed capital raise timed near the launch. Revises confidence 58 → 52; position unchanged.

### Bear rebuttal (revised confidence 20/100, moved to true no-trade)
Converges almost entirely with quant — the only friction was procedural (short-the-pop lean vs. quant's flat), and on reflection that short lean "was never backed by disconfirming evidence, only by discounting the bull case," a self-correction. Still flags: rideshare launch depends on a primary payload's manifest — schedule risk is outside ASTS's control, and "first half of August" could slide to a September re-announcement with zero new information; bull's $56 stop (~9%) undersells realistic scrub-headline gap risk on a stock that already swings 5-8% intraday — closer to coin-flip-sized than "controlled." What would change bear's mind: a firm, third-party-confirmed launch date (narrows to a specific week) flips toward tradeable-long; a slip/manifest reshuffle makes the short case evidence-based rather than a hunch. Revises confidence 25 → 20, abandons the directional short-bias.

### Quant rebuttal (confidence 71/100 in "no directional edge," position unchanged — flat)
Key self-correction: the original +12% on-time-deploy magnitude was miscalibrated to the wrong window — rideshare insertion ≠ commissioning, so the impact window through 2026-08-15 captures only launch pop-then-fade, not a fundamental re-rate. Revises the up-leg to +7% and the full distribution: on-time pop +7% (p=0.33), slip/delay -8% (p=0.42, raised for rideshare co-passenger dependency), anomaly -20% (p=0.10), muted +1% (p=0.15). Recomputed EV = -0.9% gross, ~-1.5% net after slippage — now negative. States bull's implied ~65%+ odds of an on-window up-move is too high against a ~35% base rate; states bear's "fully priced in" is asserted, not measured, and understates real event-day realized-vol optionality. Final: P(on-window up-move) ≈ 33%, confidence 71 in no edge. Not revising position — window correction turned marginal-positive EV negative, confirming rather than overturning the no-trade stance.
