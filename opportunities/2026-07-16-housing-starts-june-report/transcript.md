## Round 2 — Rebuttal

### Bull rebuttal

Concedes fully. Nothing in bear's or quant's framing gives grounds to push back — all three
land in the same place through different lenses (bull: dead catalyst window; bear: no live
anchor/no consensus; quant: near-zero-to-negative EV even under generous assumptions). The
Round-1 "thematic re-rate" hedge remains theoretical: without a consensus estimate there is
no way to even classify the print as a surprise in either direction, so the thesis can't be
built on evidence, only speculation about speculation.

What would change bull's mind: the dossier updated with (a) the actual June print, (b) the
consensus/prior it beat or missed, and (c) TOL/KBH price action since 7/17. Absent those,
this is an autopsy, not a trade.

Final: VOID, unanimous. No position.

### Bear rebuttal

Bull's "thematic re-rate if strong beat" hedge is a face-saving hedge, not a real loophole —
a conditional trade needs the condition verified before you can act on it, and bull admits
it can't be confirmed. It also quietly launders staleness into a virtue, as if five days of
silence is evidence something durable is building, when it's equally consistent with nothing
having happened, or the market already having priced and moved on without us. It would also
need its own diligence trail (permits data, rate expectations, sentiment surveys, fund flows)
that hasn't been produced.

No real disagreement with quant: converge from different directions (EV math vs. "no live
anchor, autopsy" framing) to the same conclusion — void, don't force a trade out of an event
that already happened without us.

### Quant rebuttal

Attempted to actually price bull's "thematic re-rate" hedge and it collapses on contact with
the missing data. Pricing a multi-week homebuilder re-rate requires three inputs: (1) actual
print vs. consensus (surprise magnitude), (2) current drift/positioning — how much of any beat
is already in TOL/KBH since 7/17, and (3) an entry anchor. The dossier supplies zero of the
three. The conditional is stacked on two unobserved variables — not a distribution, a
placeholder. EV = (unknown) × (unknown) − (known costs), and only the cost term has a sign,
and it's negative. A re-rate thesis is legitimate in general but requires a measurable
mispricing; here there is no number to anchor a target, stop, or size — unfalsifiable
narrative.

Final numeric verdict: ~0% probability of a positive-EV fillable trade. VOID. No disagreement
of substance remains among the three.
