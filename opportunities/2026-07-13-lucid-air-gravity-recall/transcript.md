### Quant (opus)

**Verdict up front: NO TRADE.** The signal is below both the noise floor and the cost floor.

Base rate: the academic literature on auto recalls shows the average announcement-day abnormal return is only -0.22% to -0.54%, and even a "major" recall averages ~-3% over 48 hours (UVic essay; eScholarship study). This recall — 3,627 units, powertrain, no reported injuries, no NHTSA investigation escalation — is squarely in the small/routine bucket.

LCID's daily realized volatility runs ~4-6%. A -0.3% expected recall drift is roughly one-tenth of one day's noise — statistically indistinguishable from zero. When LCID's Gravity recall hit (April 2026), the stock fell -4.76% that day, but that move was conflated with an RBC price-target cut (USD 10 to USD 8), a 29-day delivery halt, and a broader EV-sector drawdown. The recall itself was not separable from macro/sentiment.

The news is dated 2026-07-13 and already public. Impact window is 2026-08-05, ~3 weeks out, no described fresh catalyst. Scenario tree for a 3-week hold on a short LCID trade: quality narrative amplifies (30% prob, -7%), noise dominates (40%, 0%), macro/EV bounce or short squeeze (30%, +8%). E[price change] = -2.1 + 0 + 2.4 = +0.3% (drift skews up due to short-squeeze risk in a high-SI name). Short P/L = -0.3% gross. Costs on a 3-week short of a sub-USD 6, high-SI name: slippage/spread ~0.2%, borrow fee ~0.5%. Net short EV = -0.3% - 0.2% - 0.5% = -1.0%. A long is no better: E[long] ≈ +0.3% - 0.2% = +0.1%, statistically zero and unrelated to the recall thesis — just EV-sector beta.

**Proposed action:** No trade. Optimal Kelly size = 0 when edge ≈ 0. Would flip to a trade with: (a) an NHTSA formal investigation upgrade or stop-sale, (b) unit count expanding materially (10k+), (c) a datable hard catalyst inside the window, or (d) a cheap-optionality structure that pays for the tail rather than shorting into it.
