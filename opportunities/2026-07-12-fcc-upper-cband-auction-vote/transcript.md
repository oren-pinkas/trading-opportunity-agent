## Round 1 — Independent research

### BULL (opening)

What's actually being voted on, and why it should move stocks. On July 22, the FCC
votes on a draft Order (released July 1, Chairman Carr's "Big Auction" plan) to
authorize a 160 MHz auction of Upper C-Band spectrum (3.98–4.14 GHz), which combines
with the already-cleared Lower C-Band to create a contiguous 440 MHz "super-band"
spanning 3.70–4.14 GHz — one of the widest unified mid-band allocations in the world
([NewscastStudio, 2026-06-30](https://www.newscaststudio.com/2026/06/30/fcc-sets-july-22-vote-on-upper-c-band-auction-giving-broadcasters-a-firm-timeline/);
[Phillips Lytle](https://phillipslytle.com/fcc-advances-upper-c-band-auction-under-tight-statutory-timeline-july-4-2027/);
[DWT Broadband Advisor](https://www.dwt.com/blogs/broadband-advisor/2026/07/fcc-upper-c-band-draft-transition-order)).
This is not the auction itself — bidding won't happen until sometime before the
July 4, 2027 statutory deadline under the One Big Beautiful Bill Act, and winning
bidders can't launch service until December 2030 (top-75 markets) or July 2031 (rest
of country). So the fundamental cash-flow event is 1–5 years out. But the rules vote
removes multi-year regulatory uncertainty about band plan, timing, and clearing
costs — and markets have historically re-rated spectrum-constrained carriers on
exactly this kind of "starting gun" event. In the 2020 precedent, satellite incumbent
Intelsat rallied >25% in the week around the C-Band rules vote purely on clarity
about incentive-payment structure — before any auction proceeds were realized
([Benton Institute](https://www.benton.org/headlines/reactions-fcc-c-band-proposal)).
The mechanism here: (1) removal of "will Congress/FCC hit the statutory deadline"
overhang; (2) the FCC delivering 160 MHz vs. Congress's 100 MHz statutory minimum —
a bullish surprise on supply generosity; (3) sell-side/momentum flow around a
calendared, high-visibility FCC vote; (4) reinforcement of the capacity-relief
narrative amid AT&T's own flagged ~35% y/y mobile data growth.

Who benefits / cleanest instrument: T, VZ, and T-Mobile are natural beneficiaries.
Between T and VZ, favor VZ — dominant bidder in the 2021 C-Band auction ($45.45B of
$78B total — [Fierce Network](https://www.fierce-network.com/regulatory/verizon-pledges-whopping-45b-c-band-auction)),
and at $22.41 more torque than T at $188.69. SATS (EchoStar) is a weaker vehicle:
its mid-band position came from the AT&T/SpaceX divestiture (~$42.6B, FCC-approved
May 12, 2026), not C-Band, and the stock is dominated by idiosyncratic distress —
a delayed $183M interest payment and going-concern language in its 10-Q
([StockTitan](https://www.stocktitan.net/sec-filings/SATS/10-q-echo-star-corp-quarterly-earnings-report-e8bdf47b1303.html);
[Light Reading](https://www.lightreading.com/5g/echostar-delays-183m-interest-payment-as-it-waits-to-cash-in-on-at-t-deal)).
Avoid SATS for this specific catalyst.

Proposed action: build a long VZ position July 13–17, swing trade ahead of the
July 22 vote (near-certain passage under Carr's 2-1 majority), exit within 3–5
trading days after the vote (~July 27) since the durable catalyst (2027 auction
economics) is outside this window. Secondary, smaller T position as lower-beta hedge.

Additional sources: [DGTL Infra — C-band auction results](https://dgtlinfra.com/c-band-auction-results-verizon-att-tmobile/);
[DatacenterDynamics — FCC approves EchoStar's $40bn spectrum sales](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/fcc-approves-echostars-40bn-spectrum-sales-to-att-spacex/);
[Benton Institute — FCC Republican majority](https://www.benton.org/headlines/fcc-get-republican-majority-and-plans-%E2%80%9Cdelete%E2%80%9D-many-rules-possible).

### BEAR (opening)

This is a low-conviction, arguably non-event trade dressed up as a catalyst. The
July 22 vote is not an auction, not a spectrum grant, and not a proceeds event — it
is a rules order setting the framework for a competitive-bidding process Congress
already mandated over a year ago, itself not occurring until sometime before the
July 4, 2027 statutory deadline
([Phillips Lytle](https://phillipslytle.com/fcc-advances-upper-c-band-auction-under-tight-statutory-timeline-july-4-2027/)).
Actual carrier deployment doesn't happen until December 30, 2030 (top-75 markets) /
June 30, 2031 (rest) ([DWT](https://www.dwt.com/blogs/broadband-advisor/2026/07/fcc-upper-c-band-draft-transition-order)).
Any EPS/capex impact on T or VZ is 4–5 years out and contingent on winning bids in an
auction not yet scheduled.

Already priced in: telegraphed for over a year — 2025 OBBBA mandate, FCC NPRM
(FCC 25-78), Oct 30 2025 fact sheet, draft order circulated ~July 1, 2026 (three
weeks pre-vote) ([Fierce Network](https://www.fierce-network.com/wireless/fcc-tees-big-auction-c-band-super-band)).
TD Cowen's $30B–$75B proceeds estimate already public for weeks. If the market was
going to move, it would have moved on the June 30 announcement or July 1 draft, not
the procedural vote.

EchoStar/SATS is the weakest leg of any bull case: EchoStar is not a bidder in this
auction — it already monetized its spectrum earlier in 2026 (sold ~65 MHz to SpaceX,
~50 MHz to AT&T, ~$40B+, FCC-approved May 2026), and **rebranded its ticker from SATS
to ECHO in June 2026** specifically to signal exit from the spectrum-holding business
([Via Satellite, 2026-06-22](https://www.satellitetoday.com/finance/2026/06/22/echostar-swaps-sats-ticker-for-echo-to-emphasize-business-shift/);
[Fierce Network](https://www.fierce-network.com/wireless/fcc-gives-conditional-ok-echostars-spectrum-sales-att-spacex)).
If the trade is premised on SATS benefiting from this auction, that thesis is stale.

Risks to any bull case: (1) a straight party-line vote with zero new information —
Carr already previewed the content publicly; (2) 30-day comment window and potential
Section 316 protests from FSS operators (Eutelsat, SES, Telesat, Hispasat) and
aviation interests over radio-altimeter interference could slip the timeline further
right ([DWT](https://www.dwt.com/blogs/broadband-advisor/2026/07/fcc-upper-c-band-draft-transition-order));
(3) SpaceX could be an aggressive bidder in the 2027 auction, raising clearing prices
and capex for T/VZ rather than being a clean tailwind
([Motley Fool, 2026-04-08](https://www.fool.com/investing/2026/04/08/spacex-bad-news-att-verizon-t-mobile-starlink/));
(4) even a maximal $75B auction outcome is a proceeds/cost line for winning carriers,
not free upside — the 2020–21 C-band auction raised >$80B and was followed by years
of carrier capex/debt ([DGTL Infra](https://dgtlinfra.com/c-band-auction-results-verizon-att-tmobile/)).

What would change bear's mind: a genuine surprise in the July 22 order vs. the July 1
draft; concrete sell-side repricing of T/VZ price targets citing this specific vote;
confirmation EchoStar retains a direct financial stake despite divesting its
spectrum.

Sources also cited: [Chairman Carr's announcement](https://docs.fcc.gov/public/attachments/DOC-422694A1.pdf).

Genuine new information on July 22 is possible in three places, none of which land

Concede one point: an FCC docket fully telegraphed to specialists can still produce
