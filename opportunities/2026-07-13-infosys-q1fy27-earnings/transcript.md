## Round 2 — Rebuttal

### Bull rebuttal

Concedes TCS's QoQ CC growth (+0.4%) missed consensus — wrong to call it a
clean beat in R1 — but argues TCV/bookings are leading indicators (predict 2-3
quarters out) versus the lagging QoQ-growth miss; both signals are real,
pointing in different directions. Calls the bear's H-1B fee framing stale: the
fee took effect ~September 2025, roughly three earnings cycles before Infosys
set its FY27 guidance in April 2026 — likely already baked into guidance and
consensus, not a fresh surprise (absent a specific management quote to the
contrary). Accepts the quant's beat-and-fall point but reframes it: Q4 FY26 was
a guidance-*origination* event (higher stakes, wider dispersion); Q1 FY27 is
merely affirm/narrow/raise/cut within an existing band (lower stakes, narrower
dispersion) — expects smaller moves in either direction and revises its own
upside target down accordingly. Defends the entry/exit timing as intentionally
overnight (not a stale chase) to capture the full gap plus follow-through.
Flags HCLTech's 7/13-14 print (record $2.4B bookings, maintained guidance) as
unaddressed by either opponent — a second constructive peer data point.
Corrects the record: Wipro reports 7/16, *before* Infosys, so it will be a
same-cycle comp in hand by 7/23, not an after-the-fact caution flag as
characterized in R1.

**Revised:** BUY, smaller size (unhedged overnight tail acknowledged). Entry
2026-07-22T19:59:00Z @ $11.55 (unchanged). Exit 2026-07-23T19:59:00Z @ $12.05
(trimmed from $12.20). Confidence 53 (down from 58).

### Bear rebuttal

Argues the bull's TCS characterization doesn't hold: YoY (+13.9%) is
rupee-flattered and not the print-day-relevant metric; QoQ CC (+0.4% vs.
1.5-2.2% consensus) is the real miss, and TCS management's own
manufacturing/tariff-softness comments undercut the "demand improving" framing.
Calls TCV/bookings a "notoriously soft" signal — multi-year, non-binding, easily
restated. Flips the bull's framing of Wipro: not a minor comp but the absence
of a confirming data point Infosys will report without. Notes the bull never
engaged with the wage-hike or H-1B fee overhangs — a material omission given
>55% US revenue exposure. Broadly agrees with the quant's EV-based NO-TRADE,
but argues the beat-and-fall precedent should *also* be read as direct support
for the relief-rally-tail argument: a "not as bad as feared" print could trigger
an outsized relief rally precisely because positioning is cautious post-Accenture
-crash — meaning the quant's roughly-symmetric magnitude assumptions may
understate tail risk on *both* sides, reinforcing NO-TRADE for any directional
bet. Concedes the quant's mechanical/timing point independently supports
NO-TRADE regardless of fundamental view.

**Revised:** Still NO-TRADE. Confidence 65 that NO-TRADE is correct (up from an
implicit ~60-70 range); naked-short-would-work confidence lowered to 25 (from
30) — the quant's mechanical point plus the stock already sitting ~11% above
its 52-week low both cut against a clean short. Fundamentals still tilted
bearish (60), but poor risk/reward for any directional position, long or short.

### Quant rebuttal

On the TCS/HCL evidence: the bull and bear's opposing reads of the *same* TCS
print (YoY vs. QoQ CC) should make an observer *widen* toward P=0.50, not shift
up — the correct Bayesian update when two informed advocates disagree on the
sign of the same data point. HCLTech's bookings are already in the tape (INFY
didn't re-rate on it) — not a fresh catalyst. Net: P(up) unchanged at ~0.52, if
anything a mild drag from confirmed-soft TCS organic growth.

On the relief-rally tail: a real asymmetry argument, but it cuts both ways —
fattens the up-tail (washed-out positioning) *and* leaves the down-tail intact,
since the last print was -8.99% on an *actual beat*. Fatter, roughly symmetric
tails raise variance, which shrinks correct position size and reduces
attractiveness — a reason to avoid a naked short (agrees with bear), not a
reason to go long. The -8.99% beat-and-fall precedent suggests the R1
down-magnitude assumption of -4.5% was, if anything, too small.

On the mechanical blocker: **partially concedes to the bull.** The bull's
proposed entry (2026-07-22T19:59Z, pre-print) and exit (2026-07-23T19:59Z,
post-print) does not dodge the India-hours blocker — it embraces it, capturing
the full ADR gap in both directions. The trade is mechanically executable; it
should be evaluated as a binary on EV, not waved off on mechanics.

**Recomputed EV for the bull's actual structure:**
- Symmetric case (bull's optimism, ±5.6%): `EV = 0.52(5.6) + 0.48(-5.6) - 0.20
  = +0.02%` — essentially zero; breakeven P(up) ≈ 0.518, barely cleared but
  fails a variance-adjusted threshold.
- Realistic case with beat-and-fall asymmetry (up +5.0%, down -6.0%):
  `EV = 0.52(5.0) + 0.48(-6.0) - 0.20 = -0.48%`.
- Bear-tilted case (up +5.0%, down -6.5%): `EV ≈ -0.72%`.

What would flip to a directional call: P(up) ≥ 0.55 backed by an INFY-specific
catalyst (pre-announcement, large-deal TCV signal, credible guidance-raise
setup — not merely "meet"), a call-favored options skew or measurable pre-print
accumulation drift, or evidence the down-magnitude asymmetry has compressed
(the stock stops selling off on beats). None present this round.

**Revised:** P(up) = 0.52 (unchanged). Magnitudes up +5.0%/down -6.0%
(beat-and-fall-consistent). EV = -0.48%. Action: NO-TRADE. Confidence 60 (down
slightly from 61 — concedes the trade is mechanically executable, so
conviction now rests purely on negative EV rather than a hard mechanical
blocker).

### Dissent (for the post-mortem loop)
The bull never folded. The core unresolved question is a signal-timing
dispute: the bull treats strong peer TCV/deal-bookings (TCS $9.5B, HCL record
$2.4B, maintained guidance) as leading indicators predicting demand 2-3
quarters out and argues this should lift P(up) above the quant's 0.52; the
bear counters that bookings are a notoriously soft, non-binding signal and that
the lagging-but-print-day-relevant metric (TCS's sequential CC miss plus
management's own manufacturing/tariff commentary) is what the tape will
actually react to. The quant's resolution — that two informed advocates
disagreeing on the sign of the same TCS data point should widen the estimate
toward 0.50, not move it — is logically clean but untested against the actual
outcome. If the July 23 print resolves on deal-booking/guidance commentary
rather than sequential growth, the bull's leading-indicator framing was right
and the panel's "peer data is already in the tape" stance was the error to log
against. Secondary unresolved thread: whether the $100k H-1B fee is already
baked into the 20-22% margin band (bull) or remains a live, un-guided overhang
(bear) — no direct management quote found either way this round.

### Rationale
Two of three personas (bear, quant) independently converged on NO-TRADE by
Round 2. Decisively, the quant re-ran EV specifically for the bull's own
proposed structure — which the quant conceded is mechanically executable, not
blocked by India-hours reporting — and found it only ~+0.02% under the bull's
optimistic symmetric assumption (fails a variance-adjusted threshold) and
-0.48% to -0.72% once magnitudes are made consistent with Infosys's own
beat-and-fall precedent. P(up) held at 0.52 against a breakeven of ~0.52-0.55,
with no INFY-specific catalyst identified to lift it. The bull's most useful
contribution was killing the mechanical objection — the trade is a clean
binary — but that concession cuts against the bull, since it strips the
decision down to pure EV, which is negative. Per institutional lesson (DAL,
2026-07-12): when the strongest unrebutted dissent aligns with the quant's own
EV math, synthesize to NO-TRADE rather than a quarter-size directional
position.
