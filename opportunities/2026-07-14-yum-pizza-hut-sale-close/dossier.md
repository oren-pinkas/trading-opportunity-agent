Three-round panel (bull/bear/quant on sonnet/sonnet/opus, synthesizer opus). Round 1 (independent): bull proposed a long re-rating thesis (portfolio simplification + capital-return optionality as the lower-growth Pizza Hut segment is divested, Q3 close as catalyst) but had no pre-announcement price data and only a 2026-07-20 reference price (~USD 147.4-148.0). Bear and quant independently pulled near-identical price series showing YUM fell from ~USD 166 (2026-07-10, pre-announce) to ~USD 156-158 (2026-07-14, announcement day, ~-3 to -4%) to ~USD 147.5-148.1 (2026-07-20), a cumulative ~-10.2% to -10.8% decline -- the opposite of bull's predicted re-rating. Quant modeled the close itself as a fixed-price, already-signed, low-information event (80% on-time close / 15% delay / 5% terminate) yielding EV ~+0.25% gross / +0.15% net of ~10bps costs -- statistically zero. Both recommended no trade (bear 18/100, quant 15/100 confidence in a tradeable edge); bull's opening confidence was 35/100 but built on stale/incomplete data. Round 2 (rebuttal): bull independently verified the drawdown and fully capitulated -- the re-rating mechanism is falsified by price action, not confirmed. Bull considered but declined to size a 'buy the overreaction' reframe, citing no evidence to distinguish overreaction from justified repricing, and cut confidence to 20/100. Quant computed realized volatility off the price series (~40% annualized, ~2x normal QSR baseline) with a whipsaw pattern (+2.7% on 2026-07-17 immediately reversed by -4.7% on 2026-07-20) -- concluding this is elevated event-vol, not stable mean-reversion, making any reversion long Sharpe-unattractive. Quant further quantified that YUM's typical single-day earnings move (~5-7%, early-August Q2 print) is 4-10x the entire close-catalyst EV spread, meaning any position framed as a 'divestiture trade' is actually an unhedged short-gamma earnings bet in disguise; final confidence 13/100. Bear's rebuttal (retried once after an agent-side API stall) accepted quant's Sharpe critique without pushback, conceded the mean-reversion idea was 'directionally interesting, never investable,' and found no residual divestiture-specific edge once the earnings-gap risk is stripped out; final confidence 8/100, reserved only for an unsized, unevidenced possibility of close-related technical/rebalancing flow. Round 3 synthesis: rare full three-way convergence on NO TRADE, including a genuine self-correction by the bull upon seeing price data he didn't have in Round 1. The fixed-price, already-signed divestiture close carries essentially zero net expected value, and the dominant risk in the hold window (Q2 earnings, early August) is orthogonal to this catalyst. Standing follow-up for any revisit: decompose the ~10.8% drawdown into YUM-idiosyncratic versus QSR-sector/market-beta components, which no persona was able to do this round.
