## Round 1 — Independent research

### Bull (sonnet)

My read: Petrobras resuming diesel imports after a three-month gap is a real signal
of a domestic refining shortfall, not a bearish surprise -- it means Petrobras is
protecting margin by importing rather than running refineries into a supply crunch,
and it sets up the Jul 28 production/sales report as a live catalyst where the market
gets its first hard numbers on refining utilization and diesel spreads ahead of the
Aug 6 full earnings. This is a name with clean binary catalyst structure (data-driven
number 4 days out) and enough time for a pre-positioned momentum trade into the print.

Evidence:
- Rio Times Online, "Petrobras to Import Diesel in July 2026, CEO Says" (accessed
  2026-07-22): CEO confirms diesel imports resuming in July after ~3 months without
  imports, citing refining deficit domestically.
- Petrobras's own calendar: 2Q26 production and sales report due Jul 28, 2026; full
  2Q26 financial results Aug 6, 2026 -- both are known, scheduled catalysts, not
  speculative timing.
- Structural backdrop: Brazil's diesel demand is seasonally elevated in Jul-Aug
  (agricultural/harvest trucking demand, southern hemisphere winter), and Petrobras
  has historically used import flexibility to arbitrage crack spreads rather than run
  refineries at a loss -- a pattern the market rewards when refining margins are
  wide, since it signals disciplined capital allocation rather than distress.
- Reference price "USD 19.135" as of 2026-07-23T19:00Z, roughly mid-range vs its
  recent 52-week band (PBR has traded between roughly "USD 12" and "USD 20" over the
  trailing year) -- not a stock sitting at a fresh high where the catalyst is already
  priced in (contrast to the DAL lesson about a name already at a 52-week high).
- ADR liquidity is deep, options chains liquid enough for defined-risk structures.

Proposed action: Long PBR via a defined-risk call spread (~2-3 week expiry bracketing
the Jul 28 production report and partially covering into Aug 6) rather than naked
shares. Entry Fri 2026-07-24 during regular session, scale out/reassess by Jul 28
mid-day, holding only a residual position into Aug 6 if data confirms strengthening
refining discipline.

Confidence: 58/100. Real, dated, liquid catalyst with a plausible bullish read; PBR
isn't already extended into a fresh high (doesn't trip the DAL lesson). Capped
because "resumed diesel imports" is genuinely dual-edged -- could read as eroding
refining capacity/underinvestment instead -- and I don't have hard crack-spread or
utilization data to settle it, only the CEO's framing. Defined-risk structure chosen
per the NKE lesson on expressing thesis-uncertain catalysts via spreads, never naked.

timed around Jul 28 or Aug 6 on this evidence alone.

Confidence: 30/100 -- high confidence that "no clean tradeable edge exists in this
specific dossier," but overall directional confidence capped low: lacking actual
Brent/crack-spread and BRL trajectory data, the news is stale by market-reaction

## Round 2 — Rebuttal

### Bull (sonnet)

Concedes:
1. Quant's calendar correction is right -- Jul 28 is the production/sales report
   (volumes, low-surprise), not the full financial release (Aug 6). The margin-
   management catalyst is an Aug 6 story; mistimed the trigger by 9 days. Theta
   bleed between Jul 28 and Aug 6 is a real cost under-weighted in Round 1.
2. Bear's stale-news point has teeth. PBR flat at "USD 19.13"-"USD 19.14" across two
   full sessions with zero drift on a liquid, heavily-covered ADR is a reasonable
   prior that the market has already processed diesel-import volumes as
   non-material.
3. Quant's EV math is the most important thing on the table -- net EV ~ -0.25% after
   costs on a roughly symmetric distribution is a real headwind for any structure
   centered on this event, including mine.

Pushes back:
1. Quant's EV calc is built for a directional/delta-one bet, not a defined-risk call
   spread -- payoff shapes aren't the same. A capped-loss/capped-gain spread
   monetizes a fat right tail more efficiently than a straddle or straight equity
   long, IF the true distribution isn't symmetric. But needs IV-surface data before
   defending size -- doesn't have it.
2. Bear's "known seasonal/maintenance pattern" (REPLAN/RNEST) cuts both ways: if
   maintenance-driven, that's supportive of "proactive management, not distress,"
   not against it -- reduces the downside case more than the upside case.
3. Dividend/capex as the "real" Aug 6 swing factor (Bear) is fair but a different
   trade, not a rebuttal -- should show up as lower position size/confidence, not
   zero.

Verdict: Revise, not abandon. Confidence 58 -> 42, below own conviction bar for
size. Would drop the Jul 28-inclusive window and trade only an Aug-6-only spread,
hard-conditioned on live IV data (not currently available) confirming the spread
isn't negative-EV dressed in a nicer risk graph. Absent that data, sides materially
more with NO TRADE than in Round 1.

### Bear (sonnet)

exactly the DAL-lesson trap: taking an ambiguous, stale data point and constructing a
favorable narrative to justify a position. Importing instead of running refineries

a state-influenced NOC importing rather than maximizing domestic run rates during

margin economics aren't attractive right now -- a wash at best.

On Quant's EV calc: most rigorous thing on the table, largely agrees with the
negative-EV conclusion. Quant's symmetric 47/48 split assuming no directional
information content is consistent with the "already priced, flat tape" read -- a
stronger form of the Round 1 argument, not a separate one.

What both are underweighting: neither addressed the dividend/capex tail risk. Bull's
Aug-6 residual-hold plan is explicitly exposed to that binary with no discussion of
how the call spread handles a dividend-policy shock.

Does anything change her mind? No new information entered the debate. What would:
(1) actual Brent/crack-spread/BRL data showing refining economics deteriorated
enough to make "importing = distress" the dominant read; (2) evidence the Jul 28
report specifically breaks out import/export diesel figures with a magnitude
surprise vs ANP consensus; (3) any options-market signal (IV term structure, skew)
suggesting smart money is positioning ahead of Aug 6 inconsistent with "priced in."

Position: MAINTAIN, confidence 30, NO TRADE. Quant's independent EV math nudges
toward more comfort with NO TRADE -- convergence of two different methodologies (her
qualitative staleness/pass-through argument, Quant's quantitative EV-after-costs
calc) = no edge. Flags Bull's position as most likely a manufactured trade in search
of a thesis.

### Quant (opus)

Does Bull's "proactive, not distress" framing change inputs? No -- it's a narrative
label on the drift term, not the variance term, and NO TRADE is driven by
variance-vs-cost. Granting Bull's read fully -- shift split from 0.48/0.47 to
0.44/0.51 in his favor -- re-run: gross directional EV = 0.51(2.8%) - 0.44(2.8%) =
+0.196%; costs unchanged ~0.25-0.30%; net EV ~ -0.06% to -0.10%. Even conceding Bull
directionally right with a 7-point edge, the trade is still net-negative after
frictions. His thesis needs to move P(up) to ~0.545 just to break even, ~0.58 to
clear a 2:1 reward-to-cost hurdle -- nothing in "resumed imports = margin discipline"
justifies a 10-point probability shift on a 2-day-old, unpriced-reaction tape. Also:
Bull's defined-risk call spread is a premium-paying, vol-limited structure into two
events in 9 days -- he's buying the near leg's IV; if IV is bid (Round 1 concern),
he's overpaying the long strike, and the spread caps upside precisely where the
"strengthening discipline" tail would pay. He picked the one long structure most
sensitive to the IV level he hasn't priced.

Does Bear's PPI pass-through corroborate or undercut? Corroborates, and is the most
useful input in the room. If margins are largely mechanically hedged by the pricing
formula, the diesel-import headline has a smaller marginal effect on the Jul 28
number. Cuts the diesel-attributable component of the 2.8% magnitude by roughly a
third -- residual 2.8% then dominated by upstream pre-salt volumes plus Brent beta
regardless of the diesel story. PPI pass-through makes NO TRADE more robust, not
less. Parts with Bear on filing dividend/capex under NO TRADE for Jul 28 -- it's
correctly the real swing factor, but it's an Aug 6 object with near-zero information
release on Jul 28, exactly the window Bull is trading. So for that specific date, the
Bear's own biggest catalyst isn't even in the window.

Refined EV: P(down)=0.46, P(up)=0.49, P(flat)=0.05; magnitude PPI-adjusted 2.4% (down
from 2.8%). Gross EV = 0.49(2.4%) - 0.46(2.4%) = +0.072%. Costs 0.25-0.30%. Net EV ~
-0.18% to -0.23%. Still negative, tighter confidence band than Round 1 because PPI
reduced magnitude uncertainty. Nudges NO-TRADE confidence 68 -> 71.

Residual flex: genuine uncertainty remains the vol branch. Two events in 9 days is a
classic IV-elevation-then-crush setup. The only structure with a coherent thesis is
not Bull's long call spread (wrong sign on vega if IV is bid) but a short-vol/
calendar (sell the Jul 28-loaded near IV, own cheaper far-dated) to harvest the IV
crush -- flips Bull's premium-payer into a premium-collector, monetizing the
"already priced, muted-surprise" consensus Bear and Quant share. Cannot price it
without a live chain, term structure, or borrow/carry on the ADR options -- stays a
hypothesis, not a position.

Verdict: maintain NO TRADE, confidence 71 (up from 68). Bull's framing doesn't clear
costs even granting him the edge; Bear's PPI point strengthens the model by
compressing magnitude and pushing his own catalyst outside the entry window. The
only defensible trade is a short-vol structure that can't be priced -- an
unpriceable edge is not an edge.
