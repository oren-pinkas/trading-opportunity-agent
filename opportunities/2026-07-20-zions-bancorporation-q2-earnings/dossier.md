## Researched 2026-07-22T19:44:00Z

Three-round panel (bull/bear/quant) converged on NO TRADE. Real Q2 fundamentals
(fetched via WebSearch, since agent sandboxes had no live web access) showed a
genuinely strong core quarter -- NIM 3.27 percent (up from 3.17 percent YoY),
net charge-offs 0.06 percent, NPAs 0.48 percent of loans, CET1 up to 11.8 percent
-- but a headline EPS beat over 55 percent driven by one-time Visa Class B-1 and
SBIC gains, alongside a light core revenue print relative to some consensus
estimates. Stock fell about 2.6 percent after hours on the print, then partially
recovered over the next two sessions to about USD 70, converging with three
independent `toa price ZION --provider twelvedata` pulls across the panel.

Bear withdrew its Round 1 credit-deterioration and NIM-compression thesis as
directly falsified by the data. Quant's cost-adjusted EV was negative in both
directions (long about -0.39 percent, short about -0.38 percent after about
0.20 percent round-trip costs), with price sitting within about 0.8 percent of
the consensus price target -- an efficient-market fixed point. Bull's residual
case (fade the optics-driven selloff, target the fresh post-print analyst-target
cluster around USD 74.7 via a defined-risk USD 70/75 call spread) was rebutted
on the grounds that a one-time-gain-inflated beat produces no analyst
estimate-revision engine, evidenced by all three target hikes (RBC, TD Cowen,
Baird) keeping Hold/Neutral ratings rather than upgrading conviction.
