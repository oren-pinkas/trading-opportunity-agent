or the efficacy numbers themselves. Against a severe-unmet-need, anti-PD-1-refractory

## Round 2 — Rebuttals

### Bull rebuttal (confidence 50 → **42/100**)

Concedes the CRL pattern is a heavier anchor than credited in Round 1, and the
premarket-drop finding is a real ding — not proof the vote itself is a foregone
conclusion, but enough to move P(positive) down. Distinguishes bear's Lumakras
precedent (a *post-approval* confirmatory-trial question) from RP1's *pre-approval*
conditional-access question as not a clean match. Revises P(positive) to **~0.42**
(above quant's 0.35 floor, below an implicit 0.50 prior), because both CRLs objected
to design, not efficacy/safety, and durability data in a zero-alternative population
has moved oncology panels toward benefit-risk voting before.

Critically reclassifies the stub price feed from a data-quality footnote to an
**execution blocker**: cannot responsibly size options strikes/stops off a feed
40-60x removed from the real ~$10 tape. Revised action: same direction (defined-risk
calls/debit-spread), **cut size**, entry **conditional on a verified real price
reference** before any strikes are chosen — will not execute against the stub feed.

Would flip fully to the bear's side if the FDA briefing frames the voting question
around statistical/design adequacy rather than benefit-risk.

### Bear rebuttal (confidence 60 → **65/100**)

Argues the quant's premarket-drop finding is independent corroboration of the bear's
read, but concedes it doesn't fully settle the eventual-vote question — only the
market's initial reaction. Pushes P(negative) to **~0.60-0.65** (up from quant's
0.50), arguing the Class 1 (minor) resubmission classification is itself
informative: a "minor" fix category is inconsistent with genuinely resolving a
structural single-arm/heterogeneity defect; if the design flaw were fixable with a
minor response, it likely wouldn't have taken two CRLs to say so.

Concedes real ground to the bull: an AdCom vote *can* be framed around benefit-risk
rather than strict evidentiary adequacy, and unmet-need testimony has swayed panels
before even with substantial design concerns — flags this as a non-trivial minority
tail, not fully neutralized.

Revises action from "flat/modest hedge" to a small **defined-risk put/put-spread
sleeve** (mirroring the bull's capped-risk discipline), entered now, exited at/near
the vote — explicitly rejects an uncapped naked short given borrow costs and the
tail-risk asymmetry the quant flagged.

Would flip to the bull's side entirely if briefing materials/the voting question show
a benefit-risk/unmet-need framing rather than adequacy-of-evidence.

### Quant rebuttal (confidence 30→**35/100** point estimates, 75→**68/100** directional)

Corrects the bear's use of the 88% concordance stat: it is P(final action matches
vote | a vote occurred) — a **post-vote conditional**, not a pre-vote P(negative).
Using it to inflate the pre-vote probability double-counts, and it is largely
orthogonal to a trade that exits at/near the vote rather than holding through PDUFA.

Concedes the bear's underlying directional point is legitimate and **reruns the EV
table** under the bear's revised probabilities (P(pos)=0.25/P(neg)=0.62/P(mix)=0.13,
same magnitudes):

| Metric | R1 base (0.35/0.50/0.15) | Bear-revised (0.25/0.62/0.13) |
|---|---|---|
| Long gross EV | −2.75% | **−16.65%** |
| Long net (−3% RT) | −5.75% | **−19.65%** |
| Short gross EV | +2.75% | **+16.65%** |
| Short net (−3% RT −3% borrow) | −3.25% | **+10.65%** |

Under the bear's own probabilities, the long becomes a clear reject and the short
flips to nominally EV-positive. Restates the Round 1 finding plainly as a rebuttal to
the bull: the AdCom-announcement itself dropped the stock premarket — if convening
were bullish, REPL should have rallied on 2026-06-26; it fell; the bull's core signal
is empirically wrong-signed.

**Stress-tests the short**: if the up-tail is a squeeze at +120% (plausible on a
heavily-shorted, twice-rejected microcap) rather than the assumed +75%, short gross
EV drops to +5.4% / net ≈ **-0.6%** (flat-to-negative). The entire short-side edge
lives in an unverified tail-magnitude assumption, and the quant has no
short-interest, borrow-rate, or options-implied-move data to price that tail.

Conclusion: primary stance remains **NO-TRADE**, directional confidence eased to 68
(from 75) because the short is no longer cleanly dominated by no-trade — but will
**not** endorse a naked/modest short given the unpriced squeeze tail, and would only
consider a defined-risk put conditional on data that doesn't exist in this
environment (confirmed voting-question framing; options-implied-move data).

## Round 3 — Synthesis

After two rounds, the three positions cluster closer than their labels suggest around
a **bearish directional lean with no responsibly executable naked-stock expression**.
The bull will only go long on data that doesn't exist (a verified price reference
*and* benefit-risk framing). The bear wants a defined-risk put, not a naked short.
The quant's primary stance is no-trade, and its one positive-EV alternative (a
defined-risk put) is doubly conditioned on data that doesn't exist — confirmed
voting-question framing and options-implied-move pricing. Every persona's preferred
instrument is a defined-risk options structure, chosen specifically to cap the
unbounded tail that a broken price feed makes impossible to bound with stops.

This system's plan schema supports only stock actions (buy/sell/short/no-trade) with
a concrete entry/exit price — there is no options-structure field, and no trustworthy
price exists to anchor entry, stop, or target. Mapping the panel's converged view onto
that constraint: a long is clearly negative-EV and no persona endorses it at any
credible size; a naked short is the only nominally positive-EV stock position, but its
edge is entirely an unpriced-squeeze artifact on a twice-rejected, likely
hard-to-borrow microcap — precisely the uncapped tail all three personas refused to
take naked. There is no meaningful-size stock position any persona would actually put
on. The disciplined synthesis is **no-trade**, direction **none**, with the
documented short-lean and its blocking conditions recorded for the post-mortem.

**Confidence: 58/100** — set from the panel's own internal convergence: the quant's
directional confidence eased to 68 but a genuine, non-zero directional edge (bearish)
is being **declined on executability grounds** (broken feed + no options-structure
field), not because the edge nets to indistinguishable-from-zero. That is a materially
different — and more confident — situation than "we have no idea," but short of high
confidence because the short's entire nominal edge is a squeeze-tail artifact that
could just as easily argue for standing aside being *exactly* the right, and only,
call.

**Dissent (for the post-mortem):** The strongest unresolved disagreement is the
**AdCom voting-question framing fork**, tightly coupled to the **short-side
microstructure data gap** — genuinely load-bearing, not cosmetic, because it flips
the sign of the trade. If FDA frames the vote around *adequacy of evidence*, the
twice-cited structural defect dominates and P(negative) rises toward ~0.70 (bear/short
case). If framed around *benefit-risk in an unmet-need population with zero approved
alternatives*, the durable-response data (CR 15%, DOR ~21-28mo, 3yr OS 47.8%) becomes
the decision axis and P(negative) falls toward ~0.45 (bull case). Both bull and bear
explicitly agreed they would flip sides on this single, not-yet-public fact. Separately,
even granting the bear's P(negative)~0.62, the quant showed the short's entire edge is
an artifact of an assumed +75% up-tail; without short-interest, borrow-rate, or
options-implied-move data (none available), the short cannot be responsibly priced.
Post-mortem test: (a) did the FDA briefing (~2026-07-27/28) frame the question as
adequacy or benefit-risk? (b) did REPL's realized move match the assumed ±55-75% or
blow through it as a squeeze? Either answer tells us whether no-trade left money on
the table or correctly dodged an unpriceable tail.
