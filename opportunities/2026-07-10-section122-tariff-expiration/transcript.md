## Round 1 — Independent research (parallel, blind to each other)

### BULL (Catalyst-hunter, sonnet)

This is about as clean a binary catalyst as paper trading gets. The Section 122 10%
global surcharge has been a real cost overhang on import-heavy retailers since Feb
24, 2026 — roughly five months of margin compression baked into current prices. Per
the dossier, it sunsets by operation of law at 12:01am EDT July 24 unless Congress
acts. That's a hard, dated, mechanical trigger — not a rumor.

Exposure ranking:
- **BBY ($217.86)** — highest sensitivity. Consumer electronics sourcing skews
  heavily to China/Asia manufacturing, thin ~4-5% operating margin, so a 10-point
  tariff swing on landed cost is disproportionately margin-accretive when removed.
- **FIVE ($343.23)** — second-highest. Extreme-value/discretionary retailer,
  China-sourced low-ticket goods, thin-margin volume model; smaller float can move
  harder on the same headline.
- **COST ($399.37)** — least sensitive. Diversified global sourcing, private-label
  supply chains spread across countries, membership-fee-driven earnings — weak leg,
  include only for basket diversification or skip.

Proposed action: long BBY (50%) and FIVE (35%) shares/calls now through ~July 20,
holding through July 24-27 to catch post-sunset confirmation; skip or minimize COST
(15% or redeploy).

Confidence: **62/100**. Docked for: no confirmation Congress won't extend, no
options/IV data to gauge pricing-in, no granular 10-K sourcing %. Extension chatter
would kill conviction; silence through July 20 would raise it toward 75+.

### BEAR (Skeptic, sonnet)

Fade the "surcharge sunset" retail rally thesis. This is not a surprise — a
statutory 150-day clock public since Feb 24, 2026. Anyone tracking Section 122 knew
July 24 was the automatic sunset date on day one. A scheduled, calendar-certain,
law-mandated event is the textbook case of "priced in."

Risks the bull ignores:
1. **Extension/replacement risk.** Section 122 sunsetting "by operation of law"
   absent congressional extension is not the only lever — the administration could
   layer or replace via IEEPA declarations, Section 301 actions, or a new Section
   122 clock reset. A lapsed surcharge doesn't mean lapsed pressure.
2. **Magnitude mismatch.** COST/BBY/FIVE are large, diversified, multi-category
   retailers. A 10% surcharge on a portion of imported COGS is a modest
   single-digit-bps-to-low-single-digit margin effect, disproportionate to any
   realistic re-rating.
3. **Thin window.** 12 days to a binary event leaves little room for a clean
   directional trade; sell-the-news risk if consensus already expected it.
4. **Single-source dossier.** Only one trade-law newsletter as evidentiary basis —
   no corroborating USTR/Treasury notice, no earnings-call commentary from
   COST/BBY/FIVE management flagging Section 122 as a swing factor.

Falsification requires: management quantifying >20-30bps drag on a recent earnings
call, no live extension/replacement discussion, and demonstrable underpricing
(stocks flat/down into the date despite the catalyst).

Confidence in skepticism: **70/100**. Recommendation: NO TRADE, or wait for
confirmation.

### QUANT (Pragmatist, opus)

Base rate: pre-announced statutory sunsets are low-information by construction — the
market has had ~5 months to price both imposition and removal. Base rate for a
pre-announced, on-schedule regulatory expiration producing a >3% idiosyncratic move
in the ±2wk window: ~15-20%. Modal case = "sell the news / no news."

Magnitude (rough): COST <1% justified move (fixed-markup, membership-fee model
dominates); BBY 1-3% (material to thin ~4% operating margin, but competitive,
price-transparent category competes gains away via consumer pass-through); FIVE 2-4%
(highest theoretical beta, but higher extension-risk exposure and slippage).

EV calc for BBY (cleanest expression), 12-day hold, round-trip cost ~0.15%:
- Expires as scheduled (priced): p=0.70, move +0.3%
- Extended/replaced (bearish surprise): p=0.20, move -2.5%
- Clean expiry + underpriced re-rate: p=0.10, move +2.5%

EV_gross = 0.70(+0.3%) + 0.20(-2.5%) + 0.10(+2.5%) = **-0.04%**
EV_net = -0.04% - 0.15% = **-0.19%**

Asymmetry skews negative: extension/replacement risk is a live downside not
symmetric with the mostly-priced base case. FIVE similar or worse net of higher
slippage; COST EV ≈ 0 minus costs = negative.

Recommendation: **NO TRADE**. Confidence **72/100**. If forced: tiny
long-FIVE/short-BBY relative pair ≤0.25% NAV, but would pass. Data gap: no live
options-implied move or positioning data (stub feed only).

- Trigger to reconsider a small long BBY: no credible extension/replacement signal

- Kill condition: any extension/replacement headline stands the idea down

**dissent (for the post-mortem)**
Whether quant's p(extension/replacement)=0.25 is a stale prior that mechanically
decays toward 5-10% as July 20 passes in legislative silence (bull's view — would
flip EV positive on a small BBY long), or is an irreducible tail because IEEPA/301
replacement authority can be invoked with zero warning and therefore does *not*
decay with silence (quant's view, which explicitly rejects silence-as-signal). This
is the single variable that would move the call from NO TRADE to a small conditional
long.

**Rationale.** Two personas reasoning from independent methodologies converged on
NO TRADE with *rising* conviction: bear via legal/disconfirmation logic (70→78),
quant via explicit EV math (72→76). The quant's EV did not merely hold — it degraded
(-0.19% → -0.455% for BBY) after absorbing the bear's replacement-authority point,
moving away from the bull's position, not toward it. Meanwhile the bull's own
confidence fell (62→55) and sat below both no-trade confidences even at Round 1 —
the affirmative case weakened under scrutiny rather than strengthening, and bull
conceded the single-source gap and COST as dead weight, narrowing to a smaller,
later, contingent BBY-only long. The bull's best surviving argument (extension-risk
time-decay) is real but premature for July 12 — grounds to *revisit* near July
18-20 if the information state changes, not to act today. Acting now means paying
~0.15% round-trip cost against a modal sell-the-news/no-news outcome with an adverse
tail that hits harder than the favorable one.

**Verdict: NO-TRADE.** Testable post-mortem question: did the surcharge expire
cleanly with no replacement signal by July 20-24, and if so, was the silence-decay
logic vindicated, or was the lack of a move simply confirmation it was priced in all
along?
