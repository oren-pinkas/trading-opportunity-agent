## Researched 2026-07-22T19:10:00Z — NO-TRADE

Three-round panel (bull/bear sonnet, quant opus; synthesizer opus). The July
18 statutory deadline for six agencies (OCC, FDIC, NCUA, Treasury, FinCEN,
OFAC) to finalize GENIUS Act stablecoin rules had already passed by four days
when this debate ran, with no confirmed outcome (finalized, delayed, or
enjoined) available. Bull opened long COIN (target USD 180-187 off a USD
167.22 entry) on the theory that the absence of visible price damage implied
the OCC's affiliate-yield-ban threat to the Coinbase-Circle revenue-sharing
model hadn't landed adversely, and that Coinbase's signaled litigation was
itself a bullish stay/injunction catalyst. Bear called the staleness itself
the signal -- a dossier untouched four days past its own deadline implies
fizzle, an already-digested reaction, or lapsed research -- and flagged that
litigation is a multi-month tail risk, not a clean near-term catalyst. Quant
built an explicit outcome table (adverse 30% / favorable 20% / delayed 25% /
litigated-stay 25%) yielding EV on COIN of roughly -0.2% pre-cost and negative
to negligible after friction once a 60-80% priced-in discount is assumed. In
Round 2 the bull conceded the no-trade case is defensible without sourced
post-deadline data and cut to a half-size, tighter-stopped long with
confidence roughly halved (55 to 40); bear and quant held firm (76 and 74
respectively). Verdict: NO-TRADE, confidence 74. No position opened; reopens
only on a dated, confirmed rule outcome (favorable/carve-out or a granted
litigation stay) corroborated by a `toa price` pull showing COIN/CRCL have not
yet repriced, or on a confirmed adverse rule with cheap-IV options data
supporting a small (<=0.5% NAV) defined-risk CRCL put spread. Full debate in
`transcript.md`.
