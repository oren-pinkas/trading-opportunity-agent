## Round 1 — Independent Research

### Bull

Bullish thesis: this is a recycled headline-risk overreaction. HFCAA enforcement
escalated in 2021-2022, ADRs (including PDD) sold off hard on delisting fear, then the
December 2022 PCAOB determination that it had full audit access to China-based auditors
defused the entire crisis — zero actual delistings occurred. PDD lacking a HK secondary
listing (unlike BABA/JD/BIDU, which pre-emptively dual-listed) reflects an informed
management judgment that tail risk is low, not a warning sign. Fundamentals disconnect:
this is a structural/regulatory risk, not a demand/earnings risk — Temu and core
Pinduoduo commerce are untouched by the audit/listing dispute. Even in the disaster
scenario, forced delisting forces a venue change (OTC or accelerated HK listing), not an
equity-zeroing event.

Proposed action: long PDD (equity or call spread if IV has already spiked). Scale in on
weakness; if not yet down meaningfully from $84.615, start now and add on a further 5-8%
headline-driven drop. Target reversion to high-$80s/low-$90s within 4-6 weeks. Stop
below $75-78. Sizing: moderate-conviction — roughly half a highest-conviction size,
because "no HK listing" is a genuine differentiator versus BABA/JD that argues for
smaller size than a pure momentum long.

### Bear

The headline is a recycled scare from a secondary aggregation article (Yahoo Finance),
not a primary regulatory document — no PCAOB determination, no EO text, no SEC filing.
"Investor concerns resurface" is journalist framing for nervous sentiment, not imminent
policy action. Forced delisting requires either a PCAOB non-inspectable re-designation
(a years-long, publicly documented process) or a new executive order (which would leak/
be drafted for weeks before an August 1 window) — neither is evidenced here. Separately,
PDD carries genuine idiosyncratic downside independent of delisting mechanics: Temu's own
de minimis tariff exposure, and domestic China e-commerce margin compression from the
Alibaba/JD subsidy wars. Trade tension is more likely to bite PDD through tariffs and
consumer spending than through listing mechanics.

Proposed action: no new position, or at most a small tactical short (0.5-1% of book)
entered only on confirmed acceleration — a specific named EO draft, a PCAOB statement, or
a >5% single-day gap-down with volume signaling real institutional repositioning. Do not
chase the current $84.615 off this article alone. Tight stop (3-5%). Sizing small because:
no primary source, the "uniquely exposed" claim has been true for years without
triggering delisting, zero institutional lessons to calibrate, and the window (~3 weeks
to 2026-08-01) is too short for a slow regulatory process to resolve into an actual
forced delisting. Would change mind: a primary regulatory document (PCAOB determination
letter, draft EO text, SEC 6-K).

### Quant

Proposed action: small, conditional contrarian fade-the-fear — not an aggressive long. Do
