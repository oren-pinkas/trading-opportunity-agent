## Round 1 — Independent opening positions

### Bull (Catalyst-hunter, sonnet) — confidence 35/100

Thesis: sum-of-the-parts unlock as Cloud/Power Infrastructure (AI-datacenter power
business, higher growth/multiple) separates from lower-multiple legacy EMS business.
Proposed: long FLEX today (~USD 127.445), scale out partially around Form 10 filing
(est. Q4 2026), hold balance toward 2027-01-31 close. Expected magnitude +5-10% over
a multi-month window. Confidence capped by: slow-moving catalyst, no dossier data on
7/22 price action, no segment financials to size the SOTP gap, and the Form 10 leg
being non-executable today.

### Bear (Skeptic, sonnet) — confidence 78/100 that there is no clean tradeable edge

Spinoff is intent-only; Q1 2027 target is 6+ months out. Markets price announced-intent
spinoffs almost immediately, with re-rating concentrated in the first 1-3 sessions —
day+1 entry is likely already priced. Risks: (1) timeline slippage — multi-quarter
separations regularly slip 1-2+ quarters (tax structuring/IRS ruling, regulatory
review), and a slip itself could trigger a negative reaction; (2) separation/execution
costs (systems separation, dual management, debt allocation, stranded costs) routinely
eat near-term earnings, offsetting the unlock narrative; (3) single-source,
low-information-density evidence — aggregator only, no Form 10/8-K/management
commentary, unknown deal structure/pro forma financials/debt allocation; (4) no
re-anchored live entry check — at T+1 the cheap-entry window may already be gone.
Would change mind: a primary filing with pro forma financials, confirmed tax-free
structure, announced spin ratio/record date, activist involvement, or a stock price
that has not moved (an inefficiency signal).

### Quant (Pragmatist, opus) — confidence 18/100 that a positive-EV trade exists; recommends NO TRADE

Base rates (background knowledge): parent stocks historically pop ~+2-5% on
announcement day (day 0 = 7/22, already happened, missed). Real spinoff alpha
(~10-20% over 12-24 months) accrues AFTER separation completes, not during the
announcement-to-close limbo, which is typically flat/deal-risk-discounted. EV of a
day+1 long entered today: P(continuation) ≈ 0.45, magnitude ±1.5% symmetric (no
informational edge) → EV(gross) = -0.15%, EV(net after ~0.05% costs) ≈ -0.20%.
Negative. The 6-month hold to Jan 2027 is undifferentiated beta plus deal-completion
risk, not a timeable catalyst. The real spinoff-alpha trade (buy the stub
post-separation) lives after Jan 2027, outside this window. Single-source aggregator,
not an 8-K — won't size into an unconfirmed corporate action.

### Bear rebuttal — confidence revised to 82/100 no edge

Notes convergence with quant's EV math — two independent methods (qualitative

structural thesis (once Form 10 exists) but "wrong about being able to price the

### Quant rebuttal — confidence revised to 14/100 that a positive-EV trade exists

Revises EV math to be MORE negative after accounting for timeline-slippage tail risk:
adds ~15% probability of a -3.0% slippage-shock scenario (asymmetric downside, no
offsetting upside), giving EV(gross) ≈ -0.46%, EV(net) ≈ -0.51% (down from -0.20%).
Confirms the AI-datacenter power segment growth narrative is real but irrelevant to
today's tradeability absent segment financials to size the gap — that is a
post-separation (2027) thesis, not a today trade. Confirms no structure (options,
pairs, conditional entry) rescues it — the blocker is informational (single
aggregator source, no 8-K/Form 10, no spin ratio, no confirmed tax-free structure),
not instrumental. Recommendation stands: NO TRADE, size 0.

## Round 3 — Synthesis (opus)

The panel converged hard. Two independent methods (bear's efficient-repricing
argument, quant's EV math) reached the same "no edge today" conclusion, and quant's
slippage-adjusted EV is net-negative (-0.51%). The bull, after conceding day+1 entry
likely missed the cheap pop and that the sourcing is single-aggregator, retreated to
a conditional-only "buy on confirmation" stance. There is no executable positive-EV
trade today.

**Hypothesis:** A Flex (FLEX) sum-of-the-parts re-rating from the announced
Cloud/Power Infrastructure spinoff is a structurally sound but non-timeable thesis
today. The intent is already public (announced 2026-07-22) and any announcement pop
is behind us; real spinoff alpha accrues post-separation (targeted Q1 CY2027), not
during the announcement-to-close limbo. With no primary filing (Form 10/8-K), no pro
forma segment financials to size the gap, and single-source aggregator evidence,
there is no priceable, positive-EV entry right now. Direction: none. Confidence: 18
(confidence that a tradable long edge exists today).

**Plan:** FLEX, action = no-trade. Revisit trigger: a primary filing surfaces — Form
10 (est. Q4 2026) or an 8-K with pro forma segment financials, confirmed tax-free
structure, and spin ratio/record date. At that point the SOTP gap becomes sizable and
a long-on-confirmation entry can be evaluated (bull's revised path). Absent any
primary filing before Q4 2026, take no position. Also worth watching: activist
involvement, or a delay announcement (a potential negative catalyst, not a long
entry). Price anchor at debate time: USD 127.445 (2026-07-23T15:30Z), intentionally
NOT used as an entry anchor since no trade is being taken.

**Dissent (for post-mortem):** Fact-vs-magnitude priced. The bull's residual claim is
that "fully priced-in" is too strong: with no pro forma financials in existence, the
market can price the FACT of the spinoff but cannot yet price its MAGNITUDE — that
unsized SOTP gap only resolves at Form 10, leaving latent upside. Bear and quant
reject this as optimism dressed as an information gap: market efficiency dominates,
the absence of a flagged 7/22 pop in a next-day dossier is itself a negative
conviction signal (not blank space to fill favorably), and unsized does not mean
underpriced. Testable post-mortem: if FLEX drifts up meaningfully into the Form 10
filing while flat/undifferentiated before it, the bull's distinction was the correct
read and the panel was too anchored on same-session efficiency; if FLEX stays
beta-correlated and only moves post-separation (or on a slippage shock), the
bear/quant efficiency view wins.
