## Round 1 — Independent Research

### Bull (sonnet)

The settlement is real and already public: on **July 8, 2026**, the FTC and five
states (Illinois, Arizona, Michigan, Minnesota, Wisconsin) announced a settlement
with Deere & Company resolving the antitrust suit (originally filed Jan 15, 2025)
over repair-tool access. Terms: for **10 years**, Deere must give independent shops
and farmers the same diagnostic software/repair tools it gives authorized dealers;
Deere pays **$1M** toward states' legal costs; no admission of wrongdoing. (FTC
press release, ftc.gov, 2026-07; Hoosier Ag Today, 2026-07-08; NIADA dashboard
summary.)

Two things matter for the trade thesis:

- **This is Deere's second right-to-repair settlement in 2026** — it already
  settled a **$99M class-action** with farmers in April 2026 over the same issue
  (Yahoo Finance/Simply Wall St, July 2026). The market has had months to price in
  "right-to-repair is coming to Deere," so the July 8 FTC settlement is a
  *confirmation* event, not a surprise.
- **Reported market reaction to the July 8 announcement was muted**: coverage
  describes only a "modest short-term decline" in DE shares while the stock
  "remained strong year-to-date" (Simply Wall St News, July 2026). Analyst
  commentary framed the settlement as "manageable in size," with near-term focus
  on stabilizing North American large ag demand and tariff/pricing margin risk
  actually driving the stock (Simply Wall St, July 2026).

Financial backdrop is not bearish: Deere raised FY2026 net income guidance to
**$4.5B–$5.0B** (FY2026 Q2 earnings release, deere.com). Consensus rating is
**Moderate Buy** (10 buy / 6 hold / 0 sell); price targets: BofA $607.50 (Neutral),
Oppenheimer $680 (Outperform), RBC $752 (Outperform) — average ~$650-670
(MarketBeat, TipRanks, stockanalysis.com, July 2026). These target moves appear
driven by tariff/pricing/NA-ag-demand debates, not the repair settlement.

**Bull read: this is a de-risking event, not a re-rating catalyst.** The market
had priced tail risk of a large fine, injunctive relief materially denting
parts/service margin, or a drawn-out trial. Instead Deere got a bounded outcome
($1M, no damages, no wrongdoing admission, phased 10-year tool-access obligations)
that removes the overhang while leaving the core high-margin parts/service
business largely intact.

**Catalyst timing:** three distinguishable moments — (1) July 8 announcement
(largely stale by the dossier's July 12 open date), (2) **August 16, 2026** Q3
FY2026 earnings (confirmed via 8-K/deere.com) — the next real fundamental
catalyst where management quantifies compliance drag against guidance and updates
NA large-ag order books, (3) September 1, 2026 impact_window — no identified
scheduled event found; most plausibly a default lag marker, not a hard catalyst.
**My take: the tradeable catalyst is Q3 earnings (Aug 16)**, not the stale
settlement news and not a specifically-justified Sept 1 date.

**Instrument:** long DE common equity, not options — thesis is directional/
multi-week, not a binary jump; no reason to pay theta/vega on an already-diffuse
catalyst.

**Data caveat:** `toa price DE` returned $399.72 (7/8 14:30Z), $270.67 (7/11
20:00Z), $385.35 (7/12 14:00Z), $41.99 (9/1 14:30Z) — internally incoherent, does
not match real DE price history or the ~$600-775 analyst target range found via

**Proposed action:** Buy DE now (2026-07-13 to 07-17); hold through Aug 16
earnings; Sept 1 as soft exit deadline if thesis hasn't played out. Expected

**Confidence: 55/100** — qualitative case (overhang removed, guidance raised,
