## Round 2 — Rebuttal

### Bull rebuttal

Conceded the market did not crater on the actual 6/22 announcement — drops the
"not yet digested" thesis entirely. Reframes the ~12% slide as a mechanical,
time-limited post-placement overhang (deal priced $9.40; stock now trades below
deal price; overhang should clear within 1–2 weeks of pricing, likely near the
tail end of that window now).

Pushes back on quant's EV *inputs*, not method: the -0.75%/day drift is a linear
extrapolation off the tail of a temporary flow-driven move, exactly when that
flow should be exhausting; the ~5%/day vol and ~90–120% IV are self-flagged
assumptions, and a ±20pt error flips which side of "Sharpe~0" the trade sits on.
Argues 30% short interest / 2.6 days-to-cover makes the distribution a
"compressed spring" — fat-tailed to the upside — which a symmetric EV table
doesn't capture. Argues bear's own point (ban binds suppliers, not RCAT; no
confirmed halted shipments) undercuts the bear fade-thesis at least as much as it
caps the bull catalyst thesis.

**Revises** (does not concede, does not hold as-is): drops "7/15 as catalyst
deadline" framing entirely; treats any move as a pure technical/mean-reversion/
squeeze play. New entry: requires confirmation — reclaim/hold above $9.05–9.10 on
above-average volume in the first hour of 7/13 or 7/14; do not enter on a break
below $8.80. New target: $9.70–10.00 (down from $10–11), a partial gap-fill
toward the placement price, not a catalyst overshoot. New stop: tightened to
$8.60. New size: ~half of original, since the point-estimate edge is thin — this
bets on short-base convexity/fundamentals, not on the drift assumption being
wrong.

### Bear rebuttal

Treats the three-way convergence on "stale catalyst" as strong evidence the
dossier's framing (China ban as 7/15 driver) is wrong — but flags that's a
different claim from "nothing is tradeable," and notes the residual risk of
groupthink under-searching for a different real catalyst rather than the
debunking itself being wrong.

Engages bull's fundamentals list (Army SRR, Hellcat, Gauntlet II): finds bull's
original overreaction-fade thesis dead (falsified by quant's price series — no
overreaction to fade), but treats the fundamentals-momentum reframe as a real
business story, not a reason to trade *this* window — no confirmed binding
announcement date inside 7/15, small-cap milestone news is priced on the actual
announcement day not accumulated ahead of it, and P/S ~29x already reflects
"good news is coming." Flags Hellcat/Gauntlet II confirmed-date announcements as
a watch item for a **future**, separately-evaluated opportunity.

On quant's EV numbers: **explicitly retracts** Round 1's "slight lean toward
fading a pop" as indefensible — +$24 EV on $8,900 notional against a ±$620
1-sigma band with squeeze tail is noise, not a lean; the squeeze tail argues
*more* against the short than originally credited.

**Final position:** clean no-trade, no residual directional lean (stronger than
Round 1's soft lean). Would change mind on: (a) a confirmed dated binding event
(compliance deadline, enforcement action, 8-K quantifying exposure); (b) evidence
the raise overhang is fully absorbed; (c) a confirmed Hellcat/Gauntlet II
announcement date inside a tradeable window with elevated short interest still
in place.

### Quant rebuttal

Treats the three-way convergence as confirming the **catalyst axis** (no fresh
binding 7/15 event) but explicitly does not let it validate the **distribution
parameters** (vol/drift), since neither bull nor bear independently estimated
those. Notes bull's "overhang partly behind us" framing pulls E[move] toward zero
from -0.75% — *less* bearish, which shrinks edge on both sides and tightens the
case for flat rather than creating a new directional edge.

Quantifies bull's revised target explicitly: P(close ≥ +12%, the $10 target) ≈
3.4% (z=1.82 normal approx); P(≥+24%, $11 target) ≈ 0.02%; intraday touch via
reflection principle ≈ 7% (fat-tail/Student-t adjusted: ~5–9% terminal, ~10–14%
touch). Bull's revised structure (entry ~$8.90, stop $8.50/-4.5%, target
$10.50/+18%) is a ~4:1 reward:risk requiring P(win) ≥ 20% to break even —
realistic hit probability (5–14%) sits well below that hurdle: a negative-EV
lottery ticket **at this horizon**. Bull's fundamentals are real but shift a
multi-week/multi-quarter distribution, not the 7/15 one — "right instinct, wrong
expiry"; next real dated catalyst is 8/13 earnings.

On bear's fade-the-pop lean: agrees a naked short is un-sizeable (squeeze tail
dominates), but notes bear's actual proposal (conditional fade of a demonstrated
overreaction spike) is a legitimately better structure *if* a trigger fires — but
the shared finding is the catalyst is stale, so the triggering pop is unlikely to
materialize by 7/15: "no trigger, no trade." Options note: market IV (~90–120%)
implies 2-day sigma ~8.9%, *higher* than quant's realized estimate (~7%) — reads
as small-cap vol-risk-premium/squeeze-hedging demand, not the market pricing a
real event; selling premium is theoretically +EV (~1–2% notional) but small-cap
spreads (10–20% of premium) and short-gamma-into-squeeze tail risk erase/exceed
it — skip.

**Final call: unchanged, no trade (flat), refined not reversed.**
