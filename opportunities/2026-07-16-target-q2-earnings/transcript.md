**Revision:** Confidence down from ~55-58 to **~42-45/100**. No longer favors a standard directional call spread; shifts toward a much smaller, further-OTM lottery-sized position, or concedes no-trade may be the superior default — explicitly "converging much closer to QUANT's no-trade conclusion."

**Would fully abandon bullish view if:** tariff/margin risk proves bigger than what's baked into guidance; execution disruption signals in merchandising/supply chain beyond the generic reorg; the "good print, bad reaction" pattern turns out to be a multi-quarter trend, not a one-off; implied vol richness persists even with defined-risk structuring.

## Round 3 — Synthesis (opus)

### Hypothesis

**Statement:** TGT enters its Q2 print richly positioned and stretched: spot (~USD 139.59) sits ~4% above the mean Street target (~USD 133.7-133.84, "Hold" consensus) and near the top ~5% of the 52-week range, while options price a ~10.3% weekly / ~12.4% monthly implied move against an ~8% historical realized median — i.e. volatility is expensive. The most decision-relevant precedent is the immediately prior Q1 print (2026-05-20): a clean fundamental beat that still gapped down ~4-5% on margin/SG&A concerns ("good print, bad reaction"), reinforced by tariff cost inflation (~5% in affected categories) pressuring the just-raised margin guidance and a tougher Q2 comp lap (Nintendo Switch 2). Genuine company-specific top-line catalysts exist (back-to-school levers, LoveShackFancy) but none is a non-priced-in, event-specific edge. No directional bet clears the risk filter: the adverse-tail-to-edge ratio (~15x) exceeds the 7-8x no-trade threshold symmetrically for longs and shorts, and buying rich vol is negative-EV.

**Direction:** no-trade
**Confidence:** 70

### Plan

**NO-TRADE — TGT.** No entry or exit. Rationale: (a) all three panelists converged on no-trade by Round 2 (bull revised to ~42-45, explicitly converging toward QUANT's conclusion; bear held at ~43; quant raised to ~72); (b) directional long EV ≈ -1.1%, directional short EV ≈ +0.5% but disqualified by the ~15x adverse-tail-to-edge ratio; (c) long premium (straddle) is negative-EV because implied (~10.3% weekly) is rich to realized (~8%); (d) no participant surfaced a new, non-priced-in, event-specific catalyst to override the base-rate coin-flip on direction.

**Watchlist to reopen:** spot pulls back to ≤ ~USD 133.7 (mean target) → revisit bull call spread; implied vol compresses to ≤ ~8% realized → revisit bear put spread; real non-hallucinated pre-announcement data materially shifts P(up)/P(down).

### Dissent

The strongest unresolved disagreement is a magnitude-of-conviction gap on the *same* conclusion, concentrated between QUANT (~72) and BEAR (~43) — both endorse no-trade, but for structurally different reasons:

- **QUANT** treats no-trade as a hard risk-of-ruin/sizing constraint: even granting BEAR's down-tail skew, the ~15x adverse-tail-to-edge ratio and rich implied vol make any directional or long-vol expression poor regardless of thesis quality. For QUANT, the down-skew *reinforces* no-trade.
- **BEAR** holds a live, unexpressed directional lean — the "even a beat may not be rewarded" thesis is a genuinely asymmetric setup BEAR believes points down, defaulting to no-trade only because the tail math forbids sizing it, keeping a small put spread alive as a live alternative.

**Why unresolved:** the debate never tested whether a strictly defined-risk put spread (capped loss by construction) escapes QUANT's ~15x adverse-tail-to-edge constraint — that ratio was derived for undefined/directional risk, and a defined-risk instrument caps the adverse tail by construction, the same asymmetry argument BULL invoked for call spreads but neither BEAR nor QUANT fully adjudicated on the bearish side. If TGT gaps down on the print, this unadjudicated defined-risk put spread is the trade the panel talked itself out of on a risk-model technicality rather than on thesis merits. Secondary residual tension: BULL's back-to-school/LoveShackFancy catalyst was conceded as "real" by all but dismissed as "not non-priced-in" — no one quantified whether it is actually reflected in consensus, so its true edge remains assumed, not demonstrated.
