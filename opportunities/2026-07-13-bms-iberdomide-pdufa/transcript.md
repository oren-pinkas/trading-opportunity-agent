### Bear (sonnet)

Low-conviction, low-magnitude catalyst dressed up as tradeable. BTD + Priority Review + positive interim MRD data are all public for ~10 months (since Sept 2025 readout) — nothing left to trade, already priced in. PFS/OS not yet mature; approval rests on MRD surrogate, a real FDA risk axis (CancerNetwork frames it as an open question, not a formality). Registrational trial EXCALIBER-RRMM comparator (DVd) is itself a daratumumab-containing triplet, not placebo.

RRMM is crowded: BMY's own Darzalex already SOC, plus isatuximab (Sanofi), CAR-T/bispecifics (BMY's own Abecma/Breyanzi, teclistamab, talquetamab). This is a line-extension, not a new market. Materiality mismatch: BMY is $100B+ diversified pharma across Eliquis, Opdivo, Reblozyl, Camzyos, Breyanzi, Abecma, Cobenfy. Downside asymmetry: CRL/delay is the only scenario with a distinguishable price reaction, and it cuts against a long.

Proposed action: No trade. If forced, tail risk argues against shorting too — effect size too small to justify.

Confidence: High on "no trade."

Sources: news.bms.com (NDA acceptance, EXCALIBER-RRMM); biospace.com; curetoday.com; oncologynurseadvisor.com; hematologyadvisor.com; cancernetwork.com; myeloma.org.

## Round 2 — Rebuttal

### Bull rebuttal

Disagrees with bear: "already priced in" conflates data disclosure with regulatory-outcome certainty — a PDUFA date still carries its own event-risk premium even when underlying efficacy is known. Disputes bear's MRD-surrogate risk framing as standard caveat language, not evidence of unusual risk (MRD-negativity is now well-precedented as a myeloma approval endpoint).

Disagrees with quant: CRL downside magnitude (-4%/-6%) looks imported from single-asset biotech, miscalibrated for a diversified $118B pharma; a CRL is more likely a delay/manufacturing-labeling issue than a "no," and shouldn't carry that large a reaction. Would tighten CRL-case downside toward -1.5%/-3%.

What both missed: platform read-through — this is BMY's first pivotal CELMoD readout, proof-of-concept for the mechanism class underpinning follow-on pipeline assets (mezigdomide). That's optionality not captured in a single-catalyst EV calc.

What would change bull's mind: evidence sell-side has already fully priced CELMoD-platform value into consensus estimates; an AdComm call or manufacturing-site inspection flag; new FDA pushback on MRD-based approval elsewhere in oncology this year.

Revision: narrows, doesn't abandon. Confidence stays Medium. Shrinks sizing toward quant's 0.5-1.0% notional. Tightens entry window to Aug 10-14 (later/tighter) to reduce time-decay exposure to bear's "nothing left to trade" thesis on the run-up. Will not go to no-trade.

### Bear rebuttal

On quant's math: quant's own EV (+0.35% net) is inside noise for a name with 1-2% daily swings on macro alone — that's no edge, plus free downside optionality given away. Presses quant's P(approval)=0.88: if genuinely that uncertain (vs. 90-95% typical for BTD+Priority+positive-Phase-3), the "+1.0% mostly priced in" assumption is inconsistent — can't treat the outcome as high-confidence and also claim upside drift room; either it's priced (no drift left) or genuinely uncertain (downside underweighted).

On bull's platform thesis: a referendum only re-rates the stock if the vote is in doubt. At 88% consensus-priced odds, nothing about Aug 17 changes what's knowable about mezigdomide specifically — approval of iberdomide doesn't mechanistically de-risk mezigdomide's own trials. Bull conflates "strategically important to BMS" with "will cause the market to re-rate BMS" — only the second is tradeable, and peak sales estimates move on subsequent quarters of prescription data, not decision day.

What would change bear's mind: evidence of real disagreement in options market/analyst positioning (wide IV skew, dispersion in PDUFA-date price targets) suggesting the outcome isn't fully priced; a pre-PDUFA FDA action reopening the MRD-surrogate question; confirmation that comparable past BMY PDUFA line-extensions (Reblozyl, Opdualag) showed >1% average realized move.

Revision: raises confidence from High to Very High on "no trade."

### Quant rebuttal

Re-examines own EV math against both critiques. Concedes bear's pricing critique is correct: the +1.0% approved-case drift was too generous for a de-risked, fully-telegraphed regulatory checkpoint (public ~10 months) on a mega-cap; revises approved-case distribution to 0%/+0.5%, centered +0.3%.

Takes bull's platform-read-through term seriously but models it as real-and-small, not real-and-large: platform validation is a slow-burn narrative, not a decision-day repricing event — mezigdomide's own re-rating happens on mezigdomide's own Phase 3 readouts, not on iberdomide's approval. Adds it as a thin 5%-probability tail at +1.75% (cannibalized from base case), not a base-case magnitude lift.

Revised EV = 0.83(+0.3%) + 0.05(+1.75%) + 0.12(-4.0%) = +0.249% + 0.0875% - 0.48% = **-0.14% gross, ~-0.18% net**. EV flips negative — the original +0.40% was an artifact of the inflated drift assumption.

Disagrees with bull: platform thesis may be directionally right on 3-5 year fundamentals but is uninvestable on this catalyst's timeframe — no mechanism exists for the market to crystallize platform validation inside a 1-3 session exit window. Disagrees with bear only on framing: "low magnitude" and "no trade" aren't strictly identical claims, but agrees the CRL tail asymmetry is the correctly-priced piece, not the flaw.

What would change quant's mind: approved-case drift back to +1% needs evidence of non-pricing (elevated IV into the date, short-interest build, sell-side notes flagging the PDUFA as underappreciated); P(CRL) rising toward 0.20 (AdComm call, CMC flag, FDA skepticism on MRD surrogate) would flip the correct trade to a small short/put-skew rather than flat.

Revision: moves from "small long or PASS" to **PASS**. Will not short either — P(CRL)=0.12 too low to underwrite a negative-carry short, IV crush punishes long premium. Confidence raised to ~70% that there is no edge.
