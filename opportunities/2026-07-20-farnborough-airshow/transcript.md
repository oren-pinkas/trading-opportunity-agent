Day 2's order drop (58 E-Jets) is the volume catalyst — that's what moves backlog

narrative catalyst — sell-side backlog/EPS revisions, generalist media picking up
the Abra "first-ever order" storyline, and the closing-day wrap headline cycle.
These land on a lag (T+1 to T+3) because analysts need time to publish updated
notes and generalist outlets run recap pieces after specialist trade press. That's
why the plan scales in on day-3/4 order flow and exits into the July 24-27 wrap
window — betting on the second-order diffusion of day-2 news, not the news itself.

On quant's EV math: no rebuttal to the arithmetic — if net EV is -0.26% on a 0.4%
cost assumption, that's a real headwind to take seriously. Pushback is on the base
rate: "airshow catalysts decay 50%/day" is reasonable for the order-announcement
effect but doesn't model the analyst-revision/recap effect described above, which
has its own later decay curve. No hard data on that second curve's magnitude —
conceding "moderate on magnitude" confidence was probably overstated.

What would change my mind: no sell-side note upgrading backlog/EPS estimates by
July 23 EOD; ERJ failing to hold above its July 20 pre-show level on any day-3/4
dip; or a confirmed order pace at FIA2026 flat/below historical show-to-show
