## Round 1 — Independent research (blind, parallel)

### Bull (Catalyst-hunter)
This is a binary-ish, dated catalyst with a hard clock — July 24 2026, ~30 hours from now. Blitz India Media (2026-07-22T07:00Z) reports negotiators are in "final stage" talks. INFY is a pure-play US-exposed Indian IT services name — a huge share of its revenue is US-billed, so tariff/trade-framework outcomes on the India-US axis are a direct read-through to demand sentiment and rupee-cost dynamics for its US clients, even though IT services aren't the literal tariff line item — the market treats INFY as the highest-beta liquid proxy for "India-US trade thaw" headlines. Price sits at $10.67 as of 2026-07-23T17:30Z, giving us a clean, current mark to trade against ahead of the print.

Deadline-driven negotiations that reach "final stage" with a hard date typically resolve one of two ways within the window — a deal announcement (overhang removed, positive re-rate) or a last-minute extension (mild relief rally on "no rupture"). Both skew positive for sentiment; only outright breakdown is the bear case, and that's the minority scenario given how far talks have reportedly progressed.

Proposed action: long INFY common stock (or short-dated slightly-OTM call), sized small given headline-only confirmation. Entry near $10.67, target $11.20-$11.50 (~5-8%), flatten within 24-48hrs regardless of outcome. Do not average down if the headline breaks negative.

Caveat (self-flagged): single-source outlet — would want a second wire (Reuters/ET/Bloomberg) confirming "final stage" before sizing up.

### Bear (Skeptic)
The dossier's own sourcing is the first red flag: a single article from "Blitz India Media," not Reuters, PTI, or a government readout. "Talks in final stage" and "nears" are the same soft-optimism language that's accompanied every India-US trade round since 2024 without a signed deal materializing. No USTR or Indian Ministry of Commerce corroboration.

What's priced in: check where $10.67 (2026-07-23T17:30Z, twelvedata) sits relative to recent range — if IT-services names have already run up into the deadline on this headline, the asymmetry favors sell-the-news regardless of outcome:
1. Deadlines get extended far more often than met (multiple India-US slips in 2025).
2. INFY's exposure to a tariff deal is indirect at best — IT services exporter, not a goods exporter. Tariff relief on goods (textiles, pharma, autos, steel) doesn't mechanically translate to IT-services revenue/margin. The market link may be spurious.
3. ~30 hours out — any position is a binary bet on an unpredictable diplomatic timeline; even a "deal" could be a thin framework that reads as disappointment (sell-the-fact).

Proposed action: NO TRADE. If forced, a slight downside fade (small short-dated puts) rather than a long — given single-source sourcing, weak fundamental linkage, and the well-documented pattern of deadline slippage. Do not size a directional long into a binary geopolitical window on one unconfirmed article.

### Quant (Pragmatist)
Instrument mismatch: INFY is IT-services; a goods-tariff deal's transmission to it is second-order at best (broad India risk-on/off sentiment, INR — not direct tariff exposure). Single low-tier source; "final stage" framing precedes extensions as often as deals.

Base rates assumed: clean positive deal 30%, extension/vague framework 45%, breakdown 25%.
Magnitude assumed: +3% / 0% / -4% (downside asymmetry — disappointment hits harder than an anticipated positive).

EV = 0.30(+3%) + 0.45(0%) + 0.25(-4%) = +0.90% - 1.00% = **-0.10% gross**.
After costs (ADR bid/ask + slippage into an event window, ~0.3-0.5% round-trip): **-0.4% to -0.6% net**.

Process constraint: any "exit on confirmed breakdown" clause is prose only — sim has no path-dependent monitoring, so the full distribution must be priced as if held through, no stop available.

Proposed action: NO TRADE. If forced, at most a tiny (≤0.25R) short-bias tag on the downside asymmetry, but the actual call is to stand aside — negative post-cost EV, weak instrument linkage, single unverified source, untrusted-stop environment.

### Bear rebuttal

If that's the regime, it cuts both ways — an extension is a stale, already-priced-in non-event (repeated 3-4x since 2024), so its marginal sentiment pop is small and fades fast, while the tail risk (breakdown or sell-the-news on an actual deal) is comparable or larger than the stated 5-8% target. ### Quant rebuttal
Nothing in either opening moves priors materially. Takes bull's "both outcomes skew positive" head-on: this is a framing error — decomposing it shows the 45% modal (extension) bucket pays ~0%, so "one outcome pays, one doesn't, and the one that doesn't is the biggest bucket." Bull's own 5-8% target is only reachable in the 30% clean-deal branch, so bull is underwriting a target at roughly a third of the probability implied.

Reiterates the asymmetry (-4% downside > +3% upside, modal outcome zero) as a negative-skew payoff on a 30-hour binary with a hard clock — the worst kind of event to be long into, since you can't reprice as information arrives; the sim's lack of path-dependent monitoring means "flatten on my terms" is a fiction — you hold through the print.

Agrees with bear on action (NO TRADE) and on load-bearing objections (single low-tier source, instrument mismatch). Adopts bear's "sell the fact" point — even the +3% clean-deal branch may be overstated since a long-telegraphed deal is partly priced, making EV more negative, not less. Diverges from bear on the fade: a short is the same negative-skew bet with the sign flipped, now exposed to the 30% clean-deal gap-up risk — declines it too.

Revised position: **hardens** NO TRADE (not revising toward a trade). Notes two of three personas independently reach NO TRADE on the same source-quality and transmission-channel grounds. Would revisit only as a small long sized to the 30% branch if a higher-tier source confirms a signed-deal path before the close.
